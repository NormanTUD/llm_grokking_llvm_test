🔬 LLM Space-Deformation Explorer

Interactive visualizations of how LLMs deform space at each layer. NO PCA, NO dimensionality reduction — only real hidden-state dimensions.
What is this? Koch's paper proposes that transformer layers don't move token representations through a fixed space — they morph the space itself, like gravity bends spacetime in general relativity. Each HTML file below lets you interactively explore how a specific model processes a specific prompt, watching the space deformation happen layer by layer in real dimensions.

How to use: Click any link below to open an interactive page. You can zoom, pan, hover for exact values, and toggle individual tokens on/off in every chart.

🔍 What to Look For

1. Dimension Trajectories (📈): Each line is one token in one REAL dimension. Do tokens converge, diverge, or cross? Koch's Conjecture 1 says the geometry encodes semantic content.

2. Deformation Heatmap (🔥): Which tokens move most at which layers? Koch's Conjecture 4 predicts middle rows should be brightest (inner layers compute).

3. Signed Delta Heatmaps (🔵🔴): Blue=decrease, Red=increase. Can you trace the exact computation? For negation parity, look for dimensions where "not" causes a flip.

4. Geometric Invariants (📐): Coherence, expansion, deformation. Koch's Conjecture 1: Tokens should move together (coherence > 0) = field deformation.

5. Rank Test (🔬): Is this real morphing or just a global shift? Green bars = genuine per-token morphing. Red = just a bias.

6. Pair Distances (🔗): How do token relationships change? Koch's Section 5: Positive ORC = tokens converge, negative = diverge.

7. Wave Propagation (🌊): The cumulative "wave" of deformation. Koch's Section 2.3: Superposition of deformations propagating through layers.

8. Last Layer Reversal (🔄): Does the last layer undo the morphing? Koch's Conjecture 4: Last layer morphs space back toward embedding geometry.

9. Semantic Comparison (🎨): Which REAL dimensions respond to changing "blue" to "red"? Koch's Observation 2: Selective dimensional response.
